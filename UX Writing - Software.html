Enhancing empty state messages

Challenge

The previous empty state simply displayed "No data records available," offering no context or support. Users were left uncertain about what the screen meant or what they should do next.

Before

After

Solution

I reworked the empty state content to be more informative and action-oriented. The updated design introduces a clear message explaining why the page is empty, alongside guidance on what steps to take—such as adding application/storage areas or learning more through support links.

Outcome

This thoughtful messaging helped transform a dead-end into a helpful moment. It increased user confidence, reduced support tickets related to confusion, and encouraged users to complete setup tasks without hesitation.

Data record redesign

Challenge

The original data recording interface was cluttered and difficult to navigate. Vague labels, unclear button texts, and inconsistent status indicators made it hard for users to understand their next steps, often leading to frustration and delays.

Before

After

Solution

I rewrote interface content using clear, concise, and actionable language. Buttons were updated to reflect specific actions—like “Notify person in charge”—making their function immediately obvious. I restructured the layout to group related fields logically and made the current status stand out visually. To support users when fields were empty, I introduced helpful inline prompts like “No scopes added – Link scope.”

Outcome

The redesign made the data recording experience significantly more intuitive and efficient. It helped users complete tasks with greater confidence and fewer errors. The new structure also supported better consistency across multiple languages—an important factor in a global product environment.
